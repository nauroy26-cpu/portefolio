Electric fastening product line

Industry Georges Renault Before 2000

About

This product responsibility on electric fastening systems sat at the crossroads of mechanics, electronics, and real industrial usage. It was especially formative because it required thinking about the product not only for intrinsic performance, but also for operator use, robustness, and its place in a line or workshop environment. The work consisted of understanding usage needs, structuring a coherent product line, and managing trade-offs between reliability, simplicity, efficiency, and industrial deployment. It also involved introducing new sensorless measurement technologies, multi-fastener logic, and complex automation, notably for cylinder-head tightening. Another important dimension was the ability to generate useful data and statistics for quality control, improving traceability, process control, and analysis of tightening performance. The result was conclusive: this experience helped build a strong product foundation, connect technical innovation, automation, and quality, and demonstrate a real ability to turn an industrial tool into a source of performance and usable data.
